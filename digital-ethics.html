Ethics in the Age of Code

As algorithms govern more aspects of our daily lives, from the news we see to the services we receive, the ethical stakes of technology have never been higher. Digital ethics explores how power, bias, accountability, and consent operate within systems we often take for granted. Behind every algorithm is a set of choices‚Äîabout what to prioritize, whom to include, and what to ignore.

I see ethical tech not as a checklist but as a continuous, critical reflection on the impact of our work. In this context, I focus on how surveillance, automation, and data collection practices can be challenged through transparency, advocacy, and better design. Ethical coding practices, fair data representation, and inclusive research methodologies are central to building trust in technology. By aligning tech development with human values, we can create systems that not only work, but work justly.

Key Ethical Challenges

Algorithmic Bias

Systems that perpetuate and amplify existing social biases through training data and design choices.

Data Surveillance

Mass collection of personal data without meaningful consent or transparency about its use.

Automation Ethics

Replacing human decision-making with algorithms in critical areas like healthcare, justice, and employment.

Digital Divide

Technology access and literacy gaps that exclude marginalized communities from digital opportunities.
